OBJECTIVE

To use a variety of design methods while going through the design process with one participant.

The PillPal is a smart pill box and app designed to help a breast cancer patient remember to take their pills. These devices are designed to simplify her pill intake experience while keeping her engaged and accountable for her health. This was a collaborative project with Elleyce Pahang, Hannah Amaris Mei, and Xing Guo.

USER RESEARCH

While the goal during our research process was to gather as much information and insight as possible on the participant, an equally important objective was to have the participant be a co-designer. We wanted her to be part of making essential decisions that drove our final design so the product would truly meet her needs.

Design Ethnography     →     Cultural Probes     →     Participatory Workshop     →     3 Concepts     →     User Journey     →     Final Design

At the time of this project, one of our group member's (Elleyce) mom was a breast cancer patient as well as being a nurse. While we gained a lot of basic info from Elleyce, we still conducted an ethnographic interview with our participant. From this, we found opportunities in the difficulty of remembering to take medicine, human error, and miscommunication of information from the doctor to the patient. We also learned that the hospital gives patients bracelet barcodes that are scanned so nurses don't mix up patient medicine. This was essential in driving us forward in the direction of designing a better process for taking medication.

Design Ethnography     →     Cultural Probes     →     Participatory Workshop     →     3 Concepts     →     User Journey     →     Final Design

Cultural probes gave insight on pain points within our user’s daily routines and activities. It was also a method for us to gain a deeper understanding of our user’s personality and inspire design ideas. Insights we got from this:

  1. Jocelyn, our participant, is eco-friendly and likes to live in a clean environment.
  2. Wants her pill box to be fun and invite positivity.
  3. Would like a tool to guide her diet as a cancer patient.

Design Ethnography     →     Cultural Probes     →     Participatory Workshop     →     3 Concepts     →     User Journey     →     Final Design

We created a workshop consisting of three activities (making, telling, enacting) that would fully incorporate her as our co-designer; giving her the opportunity to provide valuable contribution to the design process. Insights we got from this:

  1. Automatically inputting pill taking schedule onto an app.
  2. Motivated by her family and expressed interest in wanting reminders through texts.
  3. A product that is integrated into her daily life.

TELLING: Word Association

To gain perspective on Jocelyn's definitions of convenience, accountability, and interactivity.

MAKING: Build-a-Pillbox

To make our design more personalized, we asked Jocelyn to design her own pillbox.

ENACTING: Roleplaying

We first learned about her current routine on taking pills and then had her act out how she'd take her pills differently.

At this point, we had enough development to come up with three concepts. The feedback we got was to combine both the Auto Scheduling App and the Bluetooth Connected Pill Box.
